Cincinnati Zoo & Biotanical Gardens

Maximizing a visit to the zoo

The Cincinnati Zoo needed a way to increase return traffic and improve individual exhibits in a way that would inherently promote personalized and themed exploration of the zoo. A critical consideration was how we could do both while also anticipating reduced funding for zoo staff in the near future.

Mobile Wayfinding

The goals we established to solve the two main design challenges were to increase participation at key exhibits, while at the same time understanding that a larger system needed to establish a visitor’s expectations and interest before even entering park, meet those expectations during the time spent in the park and continue to generate interest in return visits.

Persona Development

We developed personas for two children between the ages of 8–14 to focus our explorations on satisfying specific users’ needs. Both of these personas were of children that had already shown some interest in taking away some degree of knowledge from the zoo experience.

  • Jayden Koch is an impatient 4th grader that needed to be engaged visually and provided a narrative to help guide him through exhibits. A strong urge to collect drives his exploration, and key facts are kept to a minimum to enable instant comparisons between species with the mobile application.
  • Madison Clarke is at the top of her class, and is already deeply interested in animal behavior at age 14. She needs to get the most from the time she has at exhibits while visiting with her younger siblings, and the mobile app lets her keep up with zoo events and exhibits when away.
