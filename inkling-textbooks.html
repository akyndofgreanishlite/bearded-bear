Inkling

Digital textbooks for iOS and the Web

PDFs and ePub simply don’t provide a rich, device-tailored experience that can be seemlessly resumed from anywhere at anytime. To reinvision (longform) content/digital publishing, Inkling first tackled content from higher and continuing education. This complex content stood to benefit most from the combination of reflowable text and integrated rich media enabled by the next generation of web standards and mobile devices.

Translating the User Experience across Devices

As a lead interactive designer at Inkling I worked on teams of content architects, editorial staff, and developers to translate books from print volumes to structured digital content. Leading visual design or front-end development on a title-by-title basis, I brought over 30 device agnostic titles to the Inkling store.

A Modular Approach to Design

Working closely with the Art Director, I established Inkling’s Visual Pattern Library to catalog and standardize content groupings commonly found in textbooks, allowing us to scale production and reduce errors by overseas vendors.

These patterns double as templates for publishers to jumpstart their experience in Inkling Habitat, allowing authors and editors with minimal developer support to produce content that’s both beautiful and semantic.
