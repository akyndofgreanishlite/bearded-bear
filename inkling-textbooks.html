Inkling

Textbooks for all platforms

As a lead interactive designer at Inkling, I worked on in-house teams of content architects, editorial staff, and developers to translate books from print volumes to structured digital content. Leading visual design or front-end development on a title-by-title basis, sometimes responsible for both, I helped design and produce over 30 device agnostic titles, many of them currently available on Inkling.

Translating the User Experience across Devices

Seeking to reinvision complex content publishing in new ways, we first tackled higher and continuing education as markets that could benefit most from the combination of reflowable text and integrated rich media enabled by the next generation of web standards and mobile devices. PDFs and ePub simply don’t provide a rich, device-tailored experience that can be seemlessly resumed from anywhere at anytime.

A Modular Approach to Design

Working closely with the Art Director, I established Inkling’s Visual Pattern Library to catalog and standardize content groupings commonly found in textbooks, allowing us to scale production and reduce errors by overseas vendors.

These established patterns double as templates for Partners to jumpstart their experience in Inkling Habitat, allowing content creators with minimal developer support to produce content that’s both beautiful and semantic.

A New Way of Thinking About Content Distribution

While thinking of textbooks as software allowed us to move a step further than other publishers, pushing edits directly to the users with content updates, we wanted to push the envelope further. Working on pitches aimed at publishers to adopt new ways of thinking about their content and roles as curators/suppliers of knowledge, and allowing readers to drive the demand and quality of content.
