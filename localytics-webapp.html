App Analytics & Messaging

When Localytics opened their doors, mobile analytics were in their infancy, but today there are competitors vying to move event-based tracking and targeting beyond just mobile apps(have a better or more inclusive feature set, supporting a broad range of use-cases outside a single platform-specific app). Customers needed to analyze much larger segments of users across devices, and break away from micro-level analysis that didn't allow project managers and marketers to pick out patterns in (user retention, user value, etc)(with micro-segments as the smallest level of user(s) a funnel would target). Something about behavioral segments and predictive/suggest-engine.

Starting the process

Working with the Localytics team, we established a core set of objectives for the redesign of their mobile analytics web app:

  1. Create a system that was (expandible), and reduce the amount of navigation that a user would have to sift through.
  2. Treat data more like narrative content, related or more detailed (views/) of data to quickly tell the story and surfacing insights.
  3. This all needed to be more tuneable to really speak to a specific type of user (different lenses/perspectives), from product managers to marketers/developers. Refocusing their target-market from developers, to focus on product managers and marketers as a primary audience.
  4. Ultimately these analysis needed to be (condensed and viewed by managers/executives), we created a sharable executive report that would allow managers to come back to data/visualizations that were hand-picked to show the health of a single app or group of apps. This would allow the manager to save a bookmark to a given report, and return to it on a weekly (or more often) basis without learning how to use Localytics or ask for a republished version of the report. Commenting could be visible in both the report and in the original publisher’s Localytics dashboard for further (review/discussion).

Updating the Localytics brand language to match the powerful new app architecture

Brainstorming with their engineering staff, presented with concepts to begin with, and then create (visual mockups) (within the visual language of the app).
