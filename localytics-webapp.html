Localytics

App Analytics & Messaging

When Localytics was founded in 2008, significant technical understanding was required to use app analytics effectively. Today, everyone from project managers to marketers use them to track user behavior across devices and apps.

Localytics came to us to simplify a growing list of features into an app that intuitive and tunable, while improving upon its powerful data analysis capabilities and tightly integrating marketing.

Diving into the Details

Simple, Yet Powerful

Designing for mobile first, we simplified the navigation, using icons to represent the primary tasks of app management, growth, and development. This was the first step in erasing the division between analytics and marketing, emphasizing the cycle of iterating, analyzing, and refining.

Reducing the vast amount of visible controls on a chart, I grouped them by actions, placing less used features like selecting chart visualization types in corresponding popover menus.

Cross-app comparison is probably the most complex aspect/feature of the redesign, enabled through app groups and multi-app selection.

Charts as Content

One of the major breakthroughs was when I realized that the app needed to treat data like narrative content, with high-level metrics and trend previews to surface insights, enabling users to quickly find charts for in-depth examination.

Changes to the chart axis labels I felt could drastically change the experience in the app, and time-based (columns/charts) seemed like the natural place to start. By dictating the label density to always clearly show the selected date range with the minimum number of labels necessary, I helped the user focus on the visualizations.

We also implemented the chart axis controls/selection as a sentence, so users would always be able to understand and easily change the data sources and splits for a given chart. These chart formula sentences are able to be saved as presets, allowing users to iterate on charts in a comprehensive way. All chart states are perpetuated, allowing users to quickly revisit them in the future.

Accomplished through customizable dashboards, user-definable chart presets and reports.

Condensing it All

Ultimately these analyses needed to be summarized for viewing outside the core Localytics app, so an executive report was added, allowing users to package the health of a single app or group of apps for sharing to team members, executives, and investors. Commenting directly on the metrics and charts helps drive communication and understanding.
